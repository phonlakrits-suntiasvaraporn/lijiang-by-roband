✅ Before You Go

🛂 Visa

Thai nationals do NOT need a visa for China! Since March 2024, Thailand has full visa exemption.

  • Stay limit: up to 30 days per entry
  • Just bring your passport (6+ months validity)
  • No application needed — just go!

📋 China Arrival Card

REQUIRED for all foreign travelers.

  • Fill out online BEFORE your flight to save time
  • Contains: personal info, travel plans, accommodation
  • Or fill paper form at immigration
📝 Fill Online

🛡️ Travel Insurance

Get insurance that covers high altitude (4,500m+).

  • Must cover: medical at altitude + emergency evacuation
  • Recommended: World Nomads, AXA Thailand, or Allianz Travel
  • Cost: ~1,500-3,000 THB for 8 days
  • Medical evacuation from remote Yunnan can cost $50,000-300,000 USD without insurance!

✈️ Flight — Ruili Airlines

Outbound: DR5042 BKK → LJG, departs 13:50, arrives ~17:40
Return: DR5041 LJG → BKK, departs ~13:00, arrives ~14:45

  • Checked bag: 23 kg max
  • Carry-on: 10 kg max (55×40×25 cm)
  • ⚠️ No online check-in! Must check in at airport counter
  • Check-in opens 2 hours before departure

📱 Apps to Download

DO ALL OF THIS BEFORE LEAVING THAILAND!

  • WeChat — messaging + payments (link Visa/MC for WeChat Pay)
  • Alipay — payments (International Version, link Visa/MC)
  • Didi — taxi/ride-hailing (DiDi Chuxing app)
  • Amap — maps (MUCH better than Google Maps in China)
  • Trip.com — for booking train tickets
  • Google Translate — download offline Chinese pack!

See the detailed Apps & Connectivity section below for step-by-step setup.

📶 SIM / eSIM

Recommended: eSIM with built-in firewall bypass

  • Holafly — Unlimited 8-day, ~$32 USD, VPN included ⭐
  • Saily — 3-20GB, $4.49-$42.99, VPN included
  • Airalo — 5GB/7 days, ~$15 USD, partial VPN

Buy & install BEFORE leaving Thailand. Activate on arrival.

🔐 VPN Setup

Google, Instagram, LINE, WhatsApp are BLOCKED in China.

  • If using Holafly eSIM, VPN is built-in — may not need separate VPN
  • Backup VPNs: ExpressVPN (~$12.95/mo) or Surfshark (~$2.49/mo)
  • ⚠️ Install VPN app BEFORE entering China — can't download in China!
  • Install 2 VPN apps as backup (e.g., ExpressVPN + Surfshark)
  • Test it works while still in Thailand

💰 Currency & Cash

Bring 2,000-3,000 CNY per person (~9,000-13,500 THB)

  • 1 CNY ≈ 4.5 THB (Feb 2026)
  • ⭐ Exchange at SuperRich in Bangkok (best rate)
  • Bring mix of 100s, 50s, 20s, 10s — small vendors can't break 100
  • ATMs available in Lijiang & Shangri-La but sparse/none in Deqin, Feilai Si, Benzilan
  • Withdraw enough before Day 5!

🔌 Travel Adapter

Thai 2-prong plugs fit directly into Chinese sockets — no adapter needed for most devices! Three-prong plugs need an adapter.

  • Voltage: China 220V ≈ Thailand 230V — no converter needed
  • Bring 1 universal adapter + 1 multi-port USB charger to share

⏰ Time Zone

China is UTC+8, Thailand is UTC+7 — China is 1 hour ahead.

All times in this guide are China local time. Set phone to auto time zone.

🌪️ COVID / Health Entry

No requirements. No testing, no vaccination proof, no quarantine, no health declaration. Just bring your passport!

📷 Drone Warning

⚠️ Leave drone at home. China has very strict drone regulations. Many scenic areas are no-fly zones. Registration requires Chinese phone number. Not worth the hassle.

☀️ UV Warning - All Zones
Altitude = Stronger UV everywhere
UV is EXTREMELY strong at altitude — sunburn in 15 minutes even on cloudy days
Sunscreen SPF 50+ (reapply every 2 hours), UV-blocking sunglasses (essential!), wide-brim hat or cap, lip balm with SPF, moisturizer (very dry air)

💡 Travel Tips

01
Altitude Sickness
Walk slowly, drink lots of warm water, avoid alcohol for the first 2 days, don't shower on arrival night. If you feel headache/nausea, use canned oxygen and rest.
02
Essential Apps
Download Didi (taxi), Amap/Gaode Maps (Google Maps is unreliable in China), WeChat Pay (many places don't take cards).
03
Cash is King
Bring Chinese Yuan! Many small shops, markets, and street vendors are cash-only. ATMs available in Lijiang and Shangri-La but sparse in Deqin/Benzilan.
04
Canned Oxygen
Buy at Lijiang 7-Eleven or pharmacy. Bring 2-3 cans for the Jade Dragon mountain day. Available everywhere in tourist areas but cheaper in town.
05
Motion Sickness
Take pills 30 min before driving on Day 3 (Tiger Leaping Gorge) and Day 5 (Deqin). The mountain roads are extremely winding.
06
Photography Gold

🏥 Health & Safety

⛰️ Altitude Profile & Risk Levels

DayLocationAltitudeRisk
1Lijiang2,400mLOW
2Jade Dragon Summit4,506m⚠️ HIGH — highest point!
3Tiger Leaping Gorge2,600mLOW
3-4Shangri-La3,200mMODERATE
5Baima Pass4,292m⚠️ HIGH — brief
5Feilai Si3,400mMODERATE
6Benzilan2,000mLOW — relief!
7Potatso3,500mMODERATE
🟢
Mild Symptoms (Common)
Headache, slight nausea, fatigue, dizziness, shortness of breath. Action: Rest, warm water, canned oxygen, ibuprofen/paracetamol. Walk slowly. NO alcohol. Usually passes in 24 hours.
🟡
Moderate Symptoms (Concerning)
Strong persistent headache, vomiting, can't sleep, rapid heartbeat. Action: Stop activity, continuous oxygen, consider Diamox, drink water. If no improvement in 4-6 hours, descend.
🔴
Severe — EMERGENCY ⚠️
Confusion, can't walk straight, blue/grey lips, persistent coughing at rest. This is LIFE-THREATENING. Call 120 immediately. Begin immediate descent. Keep warm, continuous oxygen. DO NOT WAIT.
💊
Diamox (Acetazolamide)
Prescription needed from travel clinic. 125mg twice daily, start 24 hours before altitude. Side effects: tingling fingers/toes (normal), increased urination. DO NOT take if allergic to sulfa drugs.

🚽 Toilet Survival Guide

!
Golden Rules
ALWAYS carry tissues/toilet paper — most public restrooms don't provide it! Bring wet wipes and hand sanitizer. Many toilets are squat-style at scenic spots. Paper goes in trash bin beside toilet, NOT flushed.
💡
Pro Tip
Use the hotel bathroom BEFORE departing each morning. Don't wait until you "need" to go on mountain roads. Rest stop toilets are basic squat-style. Hotels are always western-style with amenities.

Shared dishes placed on rotating lazy Susan. Order 1 dish per person + soup + rice. Say "买单" (mǎidān) for the bill. Pay at cashier/counter (not table) — scan QR code or cash. No tipping at restaurants (can be considered rude). Don't stick chopsticks upright in rice (resembles funeral incense). Private driver tip: 50-100 CNY/day at end of service.

📱 Apps & Connectivity

DO ALL OF THIS BEFORE LEAVING THAILAND! You cannot download most of these apps once inside China.

💬 1. WeChat (微信) — Messaging + Payments

  1. Download from App Store / Google Play
  2. Register with Thai phone number
  3. Complete real-name verification with passport
  4. Link Visa/Mastercard: Me → Services → Wallet → Cards → Add Card
  5. Create trip group chat with all members

💡 Primary communication app in China. LINE/WhatsApp won't work without VPN!

💳 2. Alipay (支付宝) — Payments (Tourist-Friendly)

  1. Download from App Store / Google Play
  2. Select "International Version" on first launch
  3. Register with Thai phone number
  4. Upload passport photo + selfie for verification
  5. Link Visa/Mastercard via Tour Pass

⭐ Recommended as primary payment. Better international interface than WeChat Pay. Free under 200 CNY, 3% fee over 200 CNY.

🚕 3. Didi (滴滴出行) — Taxi/Ride-Hailing

  1. Download "DiDi Chuxing" (NOT "DiDi Rider" international version)
  2. Register with Thai phone number (need roaming for SMS code)
  3. Switch to English: Me → Settings → Language → English
  4. Link payment via Alipay (most reliable method)

💡 Drivers speak zero English. Show destination in Chinese characters on phone!

🗺️ 4. Amap / Gaode Maps (高德地图) — Navigation

  1. Search "Amap" in App Store / Google Play
  2. English version available (launched Jan 2025)
  3. Download offline maps: switch to Chinese → Me → Offline Maps → select Lijiang, Shangri-La, Diqing/Deqin → switch back to English

⚠️ Google Maps is UNRELIABLE in China — roads are offset, businesses missing. Use Amap instead!

🚂 5. Trip.com — Train Tickets

  1. Download Trip.com app
  2. Book Day 7 train: Shangri-La → Lijiang (1h 10min, ~56-70 CNY 2nd class)
  3. Need passport number to book — book 3-5 days ahead
  4. Use passport to enter station and board

🌐 6. Google Translate — Offline Translation

  1. Download the app
  2. Download offline Chinese language pack (50-150MB)
  3. Camera translation works offline — point at menus and signs!

💡 Needs VPN for online features in China, but offline mode works without VPN.

Shangri-La Dukezong
Tibetan prayer beads (mala). Yak wool scarves. Tibetan incense. Thangka paintings ($50-500+). Prayer flags. Tibetan silver jewelry.
⚠️
Beware Tourist Traps
"Pure silver" that's nickel alloy (real silver = stamped 925/999). "Jade" that's glass/resin. "Handmade" items in 20 identical shops = factory-made. Bargain: Start at 30-50% of asking, settle around 60-70%.
🎁
Best Gifts to Bring Home
Pu'er tea cakes (compact, great gifts). Dried wild mushrooms (check customs rules). Yunnan coffee. Small Tibetan handicrafts. Tax refund available for purchases over 200 CNY!

💡 Bargaining phrase: 太贵了，便宜一点 (tài guì le, piányi yìdiǎn) = "Too expensive, a little cheaper!"
